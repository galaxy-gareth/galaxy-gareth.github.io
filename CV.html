Research Interests

My main interests are the chemical evolution of the Universe and galaxy formation/evolution, specifically the role that large-scale environment plays in shaping galactic chemical evolution. My more recent work predicts the chemodynamical signature of galactic cannibalism and its impact on the growth of the Milky Way as a means of unravelling the formation history of our Galaxy. I also work on the formation of globular clusters and ultrafaint dwarf galaxies and in all my work make use of sophisticated hydrodynamic simulations using high performance computers. I am also interested in developments in computational astrophysics more broadly.
