C GARETH FEW

Hi, thanks for visiting my site.

I am a Teaching Fellow at Durham University with a background in astrophysics and science communication. I love teaching physics and science to students and the public. I have taught a variety of courses covering classical mechanics to exoplanets via lectures, practical laboratories, tutorials, and computing workshops as well as supervising research projects.

I have previously held research fellowships at the University of Hull, prior to which I completed a postdoc at the University of Exeter. I first discovered my love of computational methods in astrophysics during my undergraduate studies toward a MSc (Hons) in Physics with Theoretical Astrophysics at the University of Nottingham. After that I moved on to the University of Central Lancashire, where I was awarded my PhD on the topic of Chemodynamical Adaptive Mesh Refinement Simulations of Galaxies.

My research work is about the formation and evolution of galaxies. I use computer simulations to model the formation and evolution of spiral galaxies. My research interests include the processes which shape galaxies and the chemical elements are produced in the Universe.
